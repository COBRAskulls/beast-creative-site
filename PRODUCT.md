# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

CPG brand owners, marketing directors, and ambitious business founders researching digital agencies. Context: they're comparing options, vetting credibility, looking for proof that the agency can actually deliver. They arrive skeptical — real numbers convert them.

## Product Purpose

Beast Creative Agency's marketing and portfolio site. Goal: convert visitors into inbound leads (contact form, discovery call). Secondary: build category authority in CPG marketing and AI-enhanced production. Success = a visitor looks at the work and says "I want this."

## Brand Personality

Relentless, credible, hungry. Voice is confident without bragging — the numbers do the talking. Tone: direct, specific, never corporate. The name "Beast" earns its keep through the work, not the rhetoric.

## Anti-references

Generic agency templates. Pastel soft-sell sites. WordPress-looking layouts with stock photography and vague claims ("we help brands grow"). Cookie-cutter SaaS marketing pages. Anything that could have been made by the same agency that built a dentist's website.

## Design Principles

1. Practice what you preach — the site itself is the pitch. If the design doesn't reflect exceptional craft, no one will believe the case studies do either.
2. Specific beats vague — real numbers (22,043 entries, $4K budget, 7.03% CTR) beat any headline claim. Put proof in the reader's face early.
3. Show, don't assert — every section should demonstrate capability, not just name it. Interactive moments, bold motion, unexpected details.
4. Bold is required — this agency targets ambitious clients. Timid design signals timid thinking. Color, scale, and contrast should signal confidence.
5. AI-enhanced, human-led — lean into the AI angle (SEO + AEO, AI Content Production) without looking like the site itself was AI-generated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA. No special requirements identified. Reduced-motion support should be added to GSAP animations that aren't critical to comprehension.
